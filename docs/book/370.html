Jacket: Pictorial in very light greenish blue (171), vivid yellow (82) and black on coated white paper depicting a lyre with two clouds and a yellow star against a black and light greenish blue background; lettering in vivid yellow , very light greenish blue, reverse. Signed: EMcKK [E. McKnight Kauffer].

Front flap:
In the more than eighty years since its first publication, Palgrave’s Golden Treasury has maintained itself as a classic in its field. The constant demand for this collection of songs and lyrics and the insistent requests for The Rubáiyát of Omar Khayyám have led the editors of the Modern Library to add the Fitzgerald masterpiece to the Palgrave anthology. The poet and compiler were contemporaries, and the works by which they will always be known appeared within two years of each other. This long-cherished volume is introduced by one of the foremost anthologists of our time, Louis Untermeyer. (Spring 1944)

ML edition printed from plates made from a new typesetting. Publication announced for March 1944. WR 29 April 1944. First printing: Not ascertained. Discontinued 1970/71.
