The War in Outlinewas an abridgment of Liddell Hart’sHistory of the World War, 1914–1918(Little, Brown, 1935), which was originally published in 1930 asThe Real War(Little, Brown, 1930). The abridgment reduced the text from 635 to 285 pages. When the RH edition ofThe War in Outlinewas published Cerf wrote to Eugene O’Neill, who had lent him an earlier book by Liddell Hart: “I was so deeply impressed by that book that I swore that one day we’d get something by Hart [sic] for Random House. This is one time anyhow that a dream came true!” (Cerf to O’Neill, 21 September 1936). \tCerf does not appear to have realized that Liddell Hart was a compound surname. The author’s forenames, Basil Henry, were omitted from the title pages of Random House and ML printings and also from the jacket, andThe War in Outlinewas entered under “Hart” in ML lists and catalogues. The ML edition sold 2,481 copies between 1 May 1942 and 1 November 1943, placing it among the thirty worst-selling titles for that period. Random House offered to sell the plates to Liddell Hart after the ML edition was discontinued (Emanuel Harper to Liddell Hart, 31 October 1947). 318b.
